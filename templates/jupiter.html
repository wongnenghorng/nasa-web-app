Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, with a diameter about 11 times that of Earth and a mass 318 times greater. Composed mostly of hydrogen and helium, Jupiter has no solid surface. Its most famous feature is the Great Red Spot, a massive storm system that has raged for hundreds of years and is large enough to fit two Earths inside. Jupiter has 79 known moons, including the four large Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.

Average Distance: 778.5 million km (5.20 AU)

Orbital Period: 11.86 Earth years
