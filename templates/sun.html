Sun

The Sun is a massive, glowing ball of hot plasma at the center of the solar system, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. It has a diameter of about 1.39 million kilometers and contains over 99.8% of the solar system's mass. The Sun's surface temperature is around 5,500°C, while its core reaches 15 million°C, producing energy through nuclear fusion. This energy radiates as light and heat, making life on Earth possible. The Sun's layers include the core, radiative and convective zones, photosphere, chromosphere, and corona.
